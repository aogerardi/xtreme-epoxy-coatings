Common questions

Before you call

How much does a coated floor cost?

Pricing starts at roughly $5.85 per sq ft, but the honest answer is that the condition of your concrete drives the final number more than anything else. Grinding, crack and spall repair, moisture mitigation and stripping an old coating can move an estimate a long way in either direction. Two identical 2-car garages can quote very differently. Use the Instant Quote tool above for a ballpark, then book a free on-site measure for an exact number.

How long does installation take?

Most residential floors are ground, repaired, coated and back in service the same day. Larger or more damaged slabs may need a full day of prep plus a day for coating and cure.

Can you coat floors in the winter?

Yes, we install year-round. Garages and basements are heated or enclosed during application, and our polyaspartic top coats cure fast even in cooler temps, unlike traditional epoxy.

What's the difference between epoxy and polyaspartic?

Basic epoxy kits yellow in sunlight and can take days to cure. Our systems use a polyaspartic top coat that is UV-stable, more impact- and chemical-resistant, and ready to walk on the next day.

Do I need to prep the concrete myself?

No. We diamond-grind every slab to a clean mechanical profile and fill cracks or pitting before coating. No acid wash, no shortcuts, no peeling later.
